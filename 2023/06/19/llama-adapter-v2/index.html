Posted on 2023-06-19 Edited on 2023-06-30 In Paper , 2023 , 04 Views: Word count in article: 426 Reading time ≈ 2 mins.
Multi-modal LLM Instruction-Following Adapter Prefix-Tuning PEFT

Paper: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2304.15010.pdf

Code: https://github.com/OpenGVLab/LLaMA-Adapter

Considering the limited open-ended multi-modal reasoning ability of LLaMA-Adapter-V1(e.g., COCO Caption), LLaMA-Adapter-V2 is proposed to fully unleash the multi-modal potential of LLaMA.

Related Work

Instruction-following Models

Details can be seen in my previous note of LLaMA-Adapter V1: the 'Related Work/Instruction-Following Models' section.

LLaMA-Adapter V2 can function effectively using just language instruction data and image-text pairs, without relying on multi-modal instruction data.

Parameter-efficient Fine-tuning

Details can be seen in my previous note of LLaMA-Adapter V1: the 'Related Work/Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning(PEFT)' section.

By utilizing an early fusion strategy and bias tuning, LLaMA-Adapter V2 injects visual features into large language models, yielding impressive multi-modal instruction-following performance with only 0.04% parameters of the entire LLaMA.

Integration of Expert Systems

LLMs act as a core controller for external experts systems to boost its overall performance and capabilities.

  • Vision Tasks: visual models as experts. HuggingGPT, Visual ChatGPT, Chameleon, MMReACT and ViperGPT
  • Robotics: real-world sensors as experts. PaLM-E, Inner Monologue and Text2Motion

LLaMA-Adapter V2 stands out by integrating short, yet precise descriptions generated by LLaMA-Adapter during inference time for efficient zero-shot and training-free visual instruction understanding, setting it apart from other methods that require extensive multimodal data during training.

LLaMA-Adapter V2

Based on finetuned LLaMA-Adapter V1, LLaMA-Adapter V2 optimizes the visual projection layers on image-text pairs to ensure proper vision-language alignment. But the visual features tend to dominate the adaptation prompts, causing the instruction-following ability to deteriorate rapidly. Therefore, Early Fusion of Visual Knowledge strategy is proposed.

Bias Tuning of Linear Layers

To enhance its language instruction-following ability

The added params accounts for 0.04%(~5M) of LLaMA and its initialization is helpful for stable training at early stages.

  • \(x\): input
  • \(W\): pre-trained weights(frozen) of a certain linear layer
  • \(b=Init(0)\): learnable bias
  • \(s=Init(1)\): learnable scale factor
  • \(y=W·x\rightarrow y=s·(W·x+b)\): modify each linear layer in the Transformer
